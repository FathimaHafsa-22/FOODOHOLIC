MILK RICE

Milk rice also known as Kiribath is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made from rice. The name Kiribath (Milk rice) is given simply because it's prepared by cooking rice with coconut milk. This is a delicious breakfast preferred by many native Sri Lankan families.

AASMI

This is a traditional Sri Lankan deep-fried sweet snack which is mostly served on Sinhalese and Tamil New Years, weddings and birthdays. It is made with a combination rice flour and coconut milk, which is mixed with juice extracted from cinnamon leaves and then deep fried and atlast topped with sugar syrup mixed with food colouring.

BRINJAL MOJU

An eggplant pickle that is sweet, sour and with a hint of chilli. This dish is most commonly used in festive menus and also the famous lamprais.

POL ROTTI

Pol Rotti (Coconut Flat Bread) is a Sri Lankan flat bread which can be eaten at any time in a day as a snack or meal. It is made with the combination of flour, scraped coconut, coconut milk, water and other simple ingredients like onion and chillie.

RICE AND CURRY

A popular dish in Sri Lanka which is a well balanced meal with all nutrients. It consists of a large bowl of rice, and small portions of a vegetable curry, a meat curry, dhal curry, papadams and a sambal. This is the native food which is consumed everyday by the most number of Sri Lankans.

STRING HOPPERS

String Hoppers or Idiyaapam is a common SriLankan Breakfast staple just like Pittu and Hoppers. It is made using either steamed Wheat Flour or Roasted Rice Flour.

HELAPA

Helapa (Halapa) is a traditional Sri Lankan sweet treat. It's prepared with a combination of kurakkan flour, rice flour, treacle, cardamom, and grated coconut. It is mostly served as a traditional dessert with a cup of Ceylon tea.

KOTTU

Kottu roti alternatively spelled kothu roti, is a Sri Lankan Tamil dish consisting of chopped roti, a meat curry dish of choice along with scrambled egg. This is a popular and delicious street food available in Sri Lanka.

LUMP RICE

Lamprais is probably the most significant and sough after remnant from the Dutch colonization days in Sri Lanka. A delightfully flavourful rice wrapped in banana leaves and baked in warm oven.

HOPPERS

Hoppers are a local delicacy that are a must try food in Sri Lanka. Sri Lankan hoppers are made with rice flour, coconut milk, eggs and a little secret ingredient that makes the edges extra crispy.

POL SAMBOL

Pol sambol is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made from coconut, mostly used as an accompaniment with rice, string hoppers, hoppers and curries. It is a coconut relish, consisting of freshly grated coconut, shallots, dried whole chilies or chili powder, lime juice, and salt.

RECIPES



KOTTU

Ingredients(For Simple Egg Kottu):

  • Paratha - 1 or 2 chopped
  • Dhal - 4 tbsp
  • Egg - 2
  • Carrot 3 tbsp chopped
  • Onion - 2 tbsp chopped
  • Potato - 1 tbsp chopped
  • Green peas - 2 tbsp
  • Green Chillie - 1 tbsp crushed
  • Coconut Oil(or Any type of Oil) - 1 tbsp
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste
  • Curry Leaves - 1 sprig

Serving: For 2 person

STEPS FOR PREPARATION





  1. Cook 4 tbsp dhal with water. Add 1 tsp crushed chillies, salt, ½ tsp pepper to the dhal.
  2. Mix a little salt to the finely chopped carrot, onion, potato and green peas.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and lightly fry chopped and salted vegetables for 2-3 mins.
  4. Whisk the eggs and add it to the pan and fry it by mixing well with the vegetables.
  5. Stir fry the egg and salted vegetable for few minutes and add the chopped paratha.
  6. Once dhal is cooked, add it to the pan and mix it well for few minutes.
  7. After mixing well let it cook for few more minutes in low heat.
  8. At last Serve Warm and Enjoy.

AASMI

Ingredients(For Simple Egg Kottu):

  • Equipments
    • Pan
    • Aasmi Strainer
  • Ingredients
    • Step 1: Ladies Finger Extract
      • 300 g Ladies fingers/ Okra Fresh or frozen
      • 50 g Coconut ( Fresh or dehydrated coconut) NOT COCONUT POWDER
      • 800 g Water
    • Step 2: Batter
      • 200 g Ready-made rice flour
      • 160 g Ladies fingers/ Okra extract That was made in Step 1
      • 180 g Coconut milk
      • 1/4 tsp Salt
    • Step 3: Syrup
      • 50 g White sugar
      • 50 g White sugar
      • Drops of food Colouring of your choice ( Do not use GEL colouring)
    • Oil(For Deep Frying)

STEPS FOR PREPARATION


  • Step 1: Ladies Fingers/ Okra extract
    1. Place the coconut, water, okra in a blender and just pulse it 3-4 times, until blended. If the whole quantity doesn't fit in the blender at once then do it twice.
    2. Strain the mixture first through a normal kitchen towel, Then pass the mixture through a fine kitchen towel. The reason why it shouldn't be passed through a fine kitchen towel first is that it will be very difficult physically. So do it as a 2 step process.
    3. Leave the extract aside. ( do not make this mixture too much in advance, as the colour changes, then it will affect the colour of the Aasmi).

  • Step 2: Batter
    1. In a large bowl sieve rice flour, then add salt and coconut milk and mix well, A dough will be formed. Sometimes, you might need a little bit more coconut milk depending on the rice flour used.
    2. Once a dough is formed, then add the ladies fingers extract little by little, until it is a soft and silky batter, try running the batter through your fingers, if it flows down without breaking in the middle, then the consistency is correct. You might need more or less ladies fingers extract, depending on the consistency
    3. Once the batter is made, Heat a pot of oil on medium fire, once the oil is heated, pour in the mixture to the prepared aasmi spoon.Drop the batter into the hot oil in a circular motion first then zig-zag movements, so a circular shaped aasmi is formed, leave it for a minute and before it gets hardened, fold the round into two to make a semi-circular shape. Tighten the edge with the spoon
    4. Once the aasmi is crispy and cooked take it out of the fire and let it drain on a kitchen paper towel. Do not take the aasmi out if it is still soggy and do not keep the aasmi in too long, it will turn brown
    5. Once fully cooled down, leave the aasmi in a cool dry place covering with a cloth for at least 3 days. Don't store in an airtight container at this point

  • Step 3: Syrup
    1. Place water, sugar and food colour of your choice in a saucepan and heat until it becomes a syrup about 2-3 minutes, do not let the syrup become too stretchy as it will be hard to decorate the aasmi. It should not be too thin as well, if not the syrup will not stick to the aasmi
    2. Cool down the syrup for 2-3 minutes, Then decorate the aasmi with the syrup. draw any design with a spoon Don't let the syrup cool down too much, then it will be hard to draw patterns on it.

  • Now Enjoy

POL SAMBOL




Ingredients:

  • Grated coconut – 100g
  • Dried red chili – 8 to 12
  • Small onion / Shallots – 5 to 7
  • Lime – ½
  • Sugar – ¼ teaspoon
  • Salt – As you need

STEPS FOR PREPARATION







  1. Get ready with grated coconut, dried red chilies, onion, salt, sugar, and lime.
  2. Grind the dried red chillies with salt.
  3. Wash the mortar and pestle. Then, add the grated coconut and ground dried red chili flakes. Bash and muddle them using a pestle until they combined well.
  4. Now add sugar and bash them again.
  5. Finally, add the onions and bash them to a good mix.
  6. Taste the sambal and adjust salt if needed.
  7. Now, take off all the mixture to a plate or bowl. Then, add the lime juice and mix everything well.
  8. This is how to make an easy coconut sambal recipe at home. Serve and enjoy this Sri Lankan style pol sambol with dried red chili.

HELAPA


Ingredients:

  • 01 cup of kurakkan flour
  • 01 cup of finely scraped coconuts
  • 3/4 cups of Raga kithul treacle
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp of salt – adjust to your preference
  • 02 tbsp of sugar – adjust to your preference
  • 1 pod of cardamom – crushed
  • 5-7 fresh kenda leafs
  • about 1/4 to 1/2 cups of water

Tips:

  • If you cannot find kithul treacle to make this recipe then you can always use sugar syrup, coconut syrup, or even date paste.
  • Hard to find kenda leaves? then use banana leaves
  • If you want a firm and soft halapa them make sure to add more coconuts and all-purpose flour as kurakkan flour tends to absorb most of the moisture and tends to get hard when cooked.

STEPS FOR PREPARATION




  1. Using a medium-sized bowl, add kurakkan flour, coconuts, kithul treacle, all-purpose flour, sugar, crushed cardamom, and salt, and give it a good mix while adding water a little by little until a smooth dough forms.
  2. The dough should be a little wet and sticky, if the dough is too dry make sure to add a bit more honey or water or if it’s too wet more flour.
  3. Separate the dough into small-sized bowls – about 6-7 small bowls.
  4. Wash and dry the Kanda leaves and place one small dough ball on the leaves and flatten in (only on one side).
  5. Fold the leaf in half and press it down to seal it. Make sure to remove anything coming out of the leaf.
  6. Repeat the process for all the dough balls.
  7. Once done place the Kenda leaves in a steamer and steam for 15-20 minutes.
  8. Leave it to cool down to room temperature, unwrap it and enjoy with a nice cup of tea or pain tea.
